00Current status

Where the build stands today

The Lovable CRM scaffold exists. Supabase tables are live. Apollo enrichment is partially wired. The city-aware geo filter has been fixed (BKC, HITEC City, Koramangala, etc. all mapped). The fundamental pipeline architecture — intent signals → Perplexity research → Apollo enrich → CRM — is correct in concept but broken in execution.

Critical issue right now

Perplexity is routing through OpenRouter instead of the real Perplexity API. Every research call is either failing silently or returning low-quality results. This is the single most important fix — nothing downstream works correctly until this is resolved.

The contact loop currently treats finding one profile as success. There is no fallback chain, no contact slate concept (3–7 contacts), no P1/P2/P3 persona priority, and no "minimum viable outreach set" gate before a lead is marked ready for outreach.

02Corrected flow

The pipeline as it should work

The intended architecture is correct. The jobs are clear: Perplexity is the researcher and profile finder. Apollo is the enrichment engine only — it takes a LinkedIn URL or verified name and hydrates it with email, phone, and company data. It is not a discovery tool in this flow.

The fundamental rule

Perplexity finds the person. Apollo fills in their contact details. If Perplexity cannot find the person, Apollo falls back to its own database search — but LinkedIn-URL-first enrichment is always the preferred path because it's faster, more accurate, and cheaper on credits.

Step-by-step corrected sequence

Step
1
Intent signals trigger daily brief
  • Signal weights (set by BD team in UI) determine which companies surface
  • Scheduled Supabase function runs at 7am — calls Perplexity + ScrapingBee
  • Companies ranked by weighted intent score, top 20 surface in Daily Briefs page
  • No Apollo calls at this stage — zero credit spend until human decides
Step
2
Perplexity: company research + profile hunting
  • Triggered when BD rep clicks "Enrich & Assign" on a Daily Brief company card
  • Part A: Company research — signals with source URLs, dates, confidence scores
  • Part B (new): Profile hunting — find decision-maker name + LinkedIn URL at that company in that city
  • Search priority: Head of Ops → Facilities Manager → Workplace Manager → COO → CEO → Founder
  • Returns structured JSON with both company signals and contact profile(s)
Step
3
Apollo: enrich by LinkedIn URL (preferred path)
  • If Perplexity returned LinkedIn URL → Apollo people/match with that URL
  • Apollo returns: verified email, phone, current title, company details
  • This is the fastest and most accurate enrichment path
  • Contact written to lead_contacts with source='perplexity+apollo', confidence='high'
Step
3B
Fallback chain (if Perplexity found no LinkedIn URL)
  • Fallback 1: Perplexity second pass — "name search" prompt: find the person's name and title at [company] in [city]
  • Fallback 2: Apollo mixed_people/search with title array + company name + city
  • Fallback 3: Hunter.io domain search — email pattern match by job title
  • Fallback 4: Write lead with company data only, flag status='needs_manual_research', surface in manual queue
  • Loop continues until minimum viable contact slate reached (3 contacts) or all paths exhausted
Step
4
Outreach readiness gate + CRM insert
  • Three-score model computed: verification_score, intent_score, contactability_score
  • Outreach readiness = weighted combination of all three
  • Gate check: verification ≥ 70 AND contactability ≥ 60 → outreach_ready
  • Lead auto-assigned to BD rep by city + load balancing
  • Lead appears on Kanban with signal badge, score, and contact count

India-specific calibration

Standard verification rules assume tier-1 press coverage. Indian lease and office data doesn't work that way. A LinkedIn post from a verifiable COO saying "scouting for new Mumbai space" + a job listing for "Workplace Manager, BKC" should clear the threshold as two independent partially_verified signals. The prompt above handles this — do not apply a two-press-source rule for India.

04Apollo contact loop

From one profile to a contact slate

The success condition changes from "one person found" to "minimum viable outreach set." The loop continues until 3 usable contacts are found or all paths are exhausted.

Persona priority order

P1 — Primary targets (go here first)
Head of Administration Facilities Manager Workplace Manager Corporate Services Real Estate Manager
P2 — Secondary targets
Head of Operations Procurement Manager People Operations HRBP Office Manager
P3 — Last resort (decision-makers, not buyers)
COO CEO Co-Founder Chief of Staff EA to Leadership

Loop exit conditions

SUCCESS
3+ valid contacts found, at least 1 is P1 persona, at least 2 have a LinkedIn URL
PARTIAL
1–2 contacts found, or all P1s missing. Status = contacts_insufficient. Surfaces in rep's review queue, not auto-assigned.
HOLD
Zero contacts found after all fallbacks. Status = needs_manual_research. Appears in admin manual queue only.
Key change from current behaviour

Right now Apollo stops when one person is found — even if that person is a Founder with no email. The new loop only stops when the slate is viable. A Founder with no P1 and no email means the loop is not done. Keep searching.

05Signal schema

Evidence-backed signals, not inferences

The current signal schema stores conclusions. The new schema stores evidence. Every signal must have a source URL. If there is no URL, the signal is written to disqualified_claims, not signals. This is what makes the signal card trustworthy.

New intent_signals table columns

Table: intent_signals id uuid, pk lead_id uuid, FK → leads.id signal_type funding | hiring | office_expansion | lease | relocation | leadership_change claim text -- the specific factual claim city text or null event_date date or null -- when the event occurred source_url text -- REQUIRED. No URL = signal is invalid. source_type company_filing | company_website | job_page | tier1_news | secondary_news | linkedin_post | directory source_title text -- "Economic Times", "LinkedIn", etc. published_date date or null confidence integer 0-100 verification_status verified | partially_verified | unverified | conflicting why_it_matters text -- one sentence on managed office relevance score_contribution integer -- contribution to lead intent_score source_api perplexity | scrapingbee | apollo | linkedin | meta | manual detected_at timestamptz created_at timestamptz

Score contribution rules

-- Only applied if verification_status = 'verified' or 'partially_verified' -- If unverified or no source_url: contribution = 0, signal stored in disqualified_claims funding (verified, last 90 days) +30 funding (partially_verified) +15 hiring — workplace/facilities role +25 -- direct office signal hiring — general headcount growth +10 office_expansion (verified) +30 office_expansion (partially_verified) +18 lease signal (verified, primary source) +25 relocation signal +20 leadership_change (new Ops/Facilities) +10 -- Recency multiplier applied after base score: signal within 7 days × 1.3 signal within 30 days × 1.0 signal within 90 days × 0.8 signal older than 90d × 0.5 (or reject if source_type = secondary_news) -- Hard cap: if fewer than 2 verified signals → intent_score capped at 40
06Scoring model

Three scores, one outreach readiness gate

The current single intent score conflates intelligence quality, demand likelihood, and reachability into one number. A "78" could mean excellent verified signals with zero contact data, or weak signals with a great phone number. Split them.

Verification score
V
How trustworthy is the intelligence? Based on: number of verified signals, source quality (tier1 > linkedin_post), recency, and absence of conflicting signals.
Weight: 40%
Intent score
I
How likely do they need managed office space now? Based on: signal type contributions × recency multipliers. Capped at 40 if fewer than 2 verified signals.
Weight: 30%
Contactability score
C
How reachable is the buying committee? Based on: number of contacts found, P1 presence (×1.5), verified email presence, LinkedIn URL presence, phone number presence.
Weight: 30%
Outreach readiness = (V × 0.4) + (I × 0.3) + (C × 0.3)

Action gates

Outreach ready
Verification ≥ 70 AND Contactability ≥ 60 — appears in rep's active Kanban, auto-assigned, Draft Outreach button enabled
Manual review
Verification 40–69 — goes to manager review queue. Rep can see it but cannot start outreach without manager approval.
Hold / discard
Verification < 40 — archived, not shown in active pipeline. Admin can recover from archive.
